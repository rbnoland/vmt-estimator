# VMT and GHG Emissions Forecast Tool
### Alan M. Voorhees Transportation Center

---

## What This Tool Does

The **VMT and GHG Emissions Forecast Tool** is an interactive web-based calculator that helps planners, researchers, and policy analysts estimate how vehicle travel and greenhouse gas emissions on major roads are likely to change in the future — and why.

---

## Who It's For

This tool is designed for transportation planners, regional and metropolitan planning organizations (MPOs), environmental analysts, and policy researchers who need a straightforward, evidence-based way to project future traffic and emissions without requiring specialized modeling software.

---

## What You Can Do With It

### Forecast Future Vehicle Travel
You provide a snapshot of today's conditions — how many vehicle-miles are currently traveled on major roads, how many lane-miles of roadway exist, the current population, and today's fuel prices. You then enter projections for the future: how much road capacity, population, and fuel prices are expected to change. The tool calculates how vehicle-miles traveled (VMT) will likely shift as a result.

### Understand What's Driving the Change
The tool breaks down the projected change in travel into three contributing factors:
- **Road capacity** — adding or removing lane-miles tends to increase or decrease how much people drive
- **Population growth** — more people generally means more driving
- **Fuel prices** — higher fuel costs tend to reduce driving, and vice versa

Each factor's contribution is shown both numerically and visually, making it easy to see which forces are doing the most work.

### Project Greenhouse Gas Emissions
Beyond VMT, the tool estimates the greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions associated with that travel. You can choose from several fleet scenarios — from a typical 2024 US vehicle fleet to fleets with 30% or 70% electric vehicle penetration — or enter a custom emission factor. You can also specify how quickly the fleet is expected to electrify over time.

The tool then shows:
- **Annual emissions** in the base year and future year
- **The change in annual emissions**
- **Cumulative additional emissions** over the entire forecast horizon
- A side-by-side chart comparing projected emissions against a counterfactual (what emissions would look like if travel stayed flat but the fleet continued to electrify)

### Adjust Elasticity Assumptions
For users who want more control, the tool allows adjustment of the economic "elasticities" that govern how sensitive travel demand is to changes in road supply, population, and fuel price. Pre-set values based on published research are provided as a starting point.

### Print or Save Your Results
A print function captures all inputs, elasticity settings, and results in a clean, shareable format.

---

## What This Tool Is Not

This tool provides **planning-level estimates** based on established elasticity relationships from transportation research. It is not a full travel demand model and does not account for land use patterns, transit availability, mode shift, or localized network effects. Results are best used for high-level scenario analysis and policy screening rather than detailed project-level forecasting.
