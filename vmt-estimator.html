Methodology: Log-linear elasticity model applied to VMT on major roads (freeways, expressways, and principal arterials) — the same facility class as the lane-miles input. VMTfuture = VMTbase × (ΔLane-miles)ε₁ × (ΔPopulation)ε₂ × (ΔFuel price)ε₃. Factor-only lines hold the other two variables at base-year values. Intermediate years assume smooth annual change. Elasticity values are grounded in the induced-travel literature; for background on road capacity as a determinant of VMT see Millard-Ball & Rosen (2025), Road Capacity as a Fundamental Determinant of Vehicle Travel, UC Institute of Transportation Studies.

GHG methodology: Annual GHG (MT CO₂e/yr) = VMT (million mi/yr) × emission factor (g CO₂e/mi) ÷ 10⁶. The emission factor declines each year as the fleet electrifies: EF(t) = max(EF₀ × (1 − rate)t, 5% of EF₀). The counterfactual line shows emissions if VMT stayed at its base-year level (EF still declines); the gap between the two lines is the GHG attributable to the VMT change. Cumulative additional GHG is the sum of that annual gap over the forecast horizon. Emission factors are sourced from US EPA and FHWA fleet-average estimates.
